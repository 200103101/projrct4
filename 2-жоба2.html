Kazakhstanis food culture is traditionally centered on horse meat, lamb and dairy products. For hundreds of years, Kazakhs have been nomads on the steppes and deserts of Central Asia, raising cattle, sheep, horses and camels. The Kazakh diet is heavily influenced by nomadic life, with most cooking techniques, for example, aimed at long-term preservation.
